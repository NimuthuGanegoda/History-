# King Bhuvanaikabahu I

## Biography

<strong>Bhuvanaikabahu I</strong> (1284–1291) – Bhuvanaikabahu I relocated the capital to Yapahuwa, a fortified rock stronghold, in response to ongoing South Indian threats. His seven-year reign focused on defensive fortification and safeguarding the Tooth Relic. The capital shift reflects strategic adaptation to persistent external pressure, prioritizing security over hydraulic expansion.
