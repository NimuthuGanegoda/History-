# King Gajabahu I

## Biography

<strong>King Gajabahu I</strong> (114–136 CE) – Gajabahu I is renowned in tradition for a legendary military expedition to South India—rescuing captive Sinhalese and acquiring the sacred relic of the Tooth of the Buddha (alternative traditions vary on relic acquisition timing). His reign emphasized both martial prowess and religious patronage, reinforcing monastic establishments and tank maintenance. Gajabahu's narrative blends historical kern with heroic embellishment, illustrating how kingship integrated military valor and Buddhist devotion.
