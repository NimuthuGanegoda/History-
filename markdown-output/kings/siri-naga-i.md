# King Siri Naga I

## Biography

<strong>Siri Naga I</strong> (196–215 CE) – Siri Naga I (196–215 CE) enjoyed a 19-year reign enabling sustained hydraulic and religious investment. He reinforced earlier reservoirs, sponsored stupa restoration, and maintained legal frameworks protecting monastic autonomy. His tenure exemplifies the mature Anuradhapura model: kingship legitimized through Buddhist patronage and agrarian prosperity.
